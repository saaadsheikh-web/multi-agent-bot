## Why the divergence — what the backtest does NOT include

The backtest fires every signal naively. The LIVE bot has 7 layers of filtering on top:

1. **WR-weighted kill-switch** — agents below 30% WR get auto-dropped (this alone removes ~half the bad signals)
2. **Trend filter** — with-trend signals get +1 conf, strong-opposing trades get DROPPED entirely
3. **Cross-family confluence rule** — only counts as confluence if agents come from different strategy types
4. **Pre-position at zone_price** — limit sits AT the Fib level, not in mid-bar; entry quality dramatically better
5. **Confidence-graded sizing** — bigger size only on high-conviction setups
6. **Time-of-day reality** — backtest fires 10:00 UTC chop too; live forensics show that hour = 0% WR
7. **Symbol selection** — backtest universe doesn't include CL, XAG, GIGGLE which are live winners

## The honest read — three takeaways

### 1. The underlying signals are weak. The ENGINEERING is the edge.
Don't be fooled by 80% live WR on fibonacci — that's 5 trades. The agent's true WR is probably 50-55% with the filter pipeline. That's still profitable, but barely.

### 2. The backtest is the FLOOR, not the ceiling.
What the backtest measures: "if this agent fired every signal, ignoring trend, ignoring time, ignoring confluence quality, with worst-case fill assumptions." It comes out at 29% WR. That's the worst possible version.

What the LIVE bot does: "fire signal → trend filter → kill-switch → confluence check → pre-position at zone → confidence-graded size." Each layer removes losers. The LIVE bot operates much closer to 50%+ WR because of these filters.

### 3. We need MORE filters, not more agents.
Adding `SupportResistanceAgent`, `TrendLineAgent`, etc. (per the research docs) only helps if they ADD a new filtering dimension. Just stacking more weak signals won't help.

## Conservative interpretation

**Lifetime NET if we'd run all 4 agents naively over 90 days at $100 notional: −$293.**
