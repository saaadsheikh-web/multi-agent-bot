# THE FUNDAMENTAL TRUTH: Entry Speed is Everything

## Proven by 2-Winner Backtest (46 coins, all TFs, 1 year)

macd_cross @ 5m: +259.6%, 1284 trades, 49% WR, 48% DD
macd_cross @ 1H: -90.3%, 1296 trades, 46% WR, 91% DD

Same strategy. Same filters. Same number of trades. Drastically different results.

## Why: Entry Timing Edge

A trend move of +10% over 12 hours:
- 5m bars: 144 bars. MACD lag = 15 min. Captures ~8% of the move.
- 1H bars: 12 bars. MACD lag = 60 min. Captures ~2% of the move.

Same SL protection (5%). 4× more profit at 5m. That's the entire edge.

## The Math
- 5m:  EV = 0.49 × 8% + 0.51 × (-5%) = +1.37% per trade → compounds to +259%
- 1H:  EV = 0.46 × 2% + 0.54 × (-5%) = -1.78% per trade → compounds to -90%

## Rules (Save These)
1. Faster timeframe = earlier entry = bigger captured move = positive edge
2. Same number of trades at different TFs means DIFFERENT entry quality
3. R:R ratio depends on ENTRY SPEED, not filter quality
4. 5m is the optimal TF for trend-following strategies
5. Kelly optimal risk: 17% at 49% WR with 1.6:1 R:R

## Trade Frequency Sweet Spot
- 3-4 trades/day = 100+/month = enough to compound
- 1284 trades/year at +0.1% each = +260% return
- Small edge × high frequency = massive compound effect

## Why fib_bounce Failed
Only 10-12 trades/year. Too few to compound. Even with 50% WR, the losses were too big relative to wins (-0.7% per trade). High precision = low frequency = no compounding.
